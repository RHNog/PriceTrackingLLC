# Intellectual Property Strategy

This document defines information classifications for Project Phronesis partnership and business conversations.

Project Phronesis is the internal engineering initiative responsible for building an evidence-driven decision intelligence platform for collectible markets. The commercial product name remains undecided.

## Classification Model

Information is classified as:

1. Public
2. Partner
3. Internal
4. Proprietary

Use the most restrictive reasonable classification when uncertain.

## Public

### Purpose

Public information supports broad understanding of the platform's vision, values, responsible ecosystem posture, and customer value without exposing sensitive implementation details.

### Examples

- Vision.
- Mission.
- Product goals.
- Project Phronesis engineering identity.
- Brand philosophy at a high level.
- Responsible API philosophy.
- Evidence-driven decision intelligence positioning.
- High-level platform categories.
- General customer problems.
- General partnership intent.

### Disclosure Policy

May be shared publicly in websites, introductory emails, pitch materials, high-level partnership conversations, public documentation, and non-confidential discussions.

Public information should avoid implying that Project Phronesis is the commercial brand.

### Storage Expectations

Can be stored in public documentation, public-facing decks, website copy, and non-confidential partner materials.

### NDA Consideration

NDA is not required for Public information.

## Partner

### Purpose

Partner information supports meaningful integration conversations without revealing proprietary reasoning systems or defensible internal methods.

### Examples

- Integration architecture at a high level.
- API usage model.
- Responsible request patterns.
- Targeted provider request philosophy.
- Repository caching at a conceptual level.
- Coverage-aware refresh at a conceptual level.
- Provider diagnostics at a conceptual level.
- Development replay as a high-level API-conservation practice.
- Technical workflows that explain partner touchpoints.
- Proposed partnership goals and operating model.

### Disclosure Policy

May be shared in serious partner conversations when there is legitimate mutual interest. Share only enough technical detail to explain responsible usage, integration fit, and operational trust.

Do not disclose algorithms, weights, calibration methods, benchmark datasets, or proprietary internal implementation.

### Storage Expectations

Store in partner-specific folders, controlled documents, or non-public business documentation. Mark documents as Partner where appropriate.

### NDA Consideration

Consider NDA when:

- Technical workflow details become specific.
- Partner-specific integration assumptions are discussed.
- API request volumes or architecture commitments are discussed.
- Partner materials include non-public partner information.

## Internal

### Purpose

Internal information supports team planning, architecture, roadmap, calibration planning, operations, and platform development.

### Examples

- Engineering roadmap.
- Product roadmap before public commitment.
- Calibration methodology at a planning level.
- Replay infrastructure details.
- Repository implementation details.
- Provider evaluation notes.
- Internal platform limitations.
- Strategic sequencing.
- Non-public business strategy.

### Disclosure Policy

Internal information should remain within the team unless approved for partner disclosure. It may be summarized externally only after removing implementation details and proprietary methods.

### Storage Expectations

Store in private repositories, private docs, internal planning systems, or controlled workspace locations.

### NDA Consideration

NDA should be considered before sharing Internal information externally. Even under NDA, disclose only the minimum needed for the conversation.

## Proprietary

### Purpose

Proprietary information protects the core defensibility, reasoning methods, data assets, and competitive advantage of Project Phronesis.

### Examples

- Market Intelligence Engine algorithms.
- Asset Assessment methodology.
- Calibration weights.
- Negotiation logic.
- Strategy logic.
- Internal heuristics.
- Historical repository contents.
- Benchmark datasets.
- Provider observation library.
- Provider replay implementation details.
- Market signal derivation details.
- Evidence scoring methods.
- Business decision logic.
- Outcome-based calibration data.

### Disclosure Policy

Do not disclose externally unless there is explicit strategic approval, legal review, and an appropriate signed NDA or stronger agreement.

Some Proprietary information should never be disclosed externally, even under NDA, unless a future partnership or transaction specifically requires it and leadership approves.

### Storage Expectations

Store only in private repositories or controlled internal systems. Avoid putting Proprietary details into broadly shared decks, emails, or public docs.

### NDA Consideration

NDA is required before any Proprietary disclosure is considered. NDA alone does not mean disclosure is appropriate.

## Disclosure Summary

| Classification | Can Share Publicly | Can Share In First Conversation | NDA Recommended | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Public | Yes | Yes | No | Vision, mission, product goals, responsible API philosophy |
| Partner | No | Sometimes | Sometimes | Integration architecture, API usage model, technical workflows |
| Internal | No | No | Usually | Engineering roadmap, calibration methodology, replay infrastructure |
| Proprietary | No | No | Required before consideration | Market Intelligence Engine, Assessment methodology, weights, benchmark datasets |

## Practical Rule

Business value should be transparent.

Implementation details should remain confidential.

Partners should understand why the platform matters and how it behaves responsibly without receiving the internal machinery that makes it defensible.
