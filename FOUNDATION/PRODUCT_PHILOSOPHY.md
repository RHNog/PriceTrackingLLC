# Product Philosophy

Project Phronesis is not trying to make users click faster for its own sake.

It is trying to help users decide better.

## Core Belief

The product should help users think clearly under real-world conditions.

This matters because professional collectible-market work is often fast, imperfect, and consequential. A product that hides uncertainty may feel smooth but can make users less responsible. Project Phronesis should reduce friction without removing judgment.

## Product Principles

### Judgment Before Automation

Automation should support human judgment, not replace it.

The product should help users understand why an action is reasonable. It should not pressure users into blind acceptance.

### Calm Under Pressure

The product should feel calm during professional workflows.

Calm does not mean empty. It means organized, readable, stable, and honest. Users should be able to scan the interface, understand what matters, and act without fighting the system.

### Current Capability Before Future Promise

The product must clearly distinguish what exists now from what is future vision.

This matters for trust. A user, partner, or investor should never need to guess whether a workflow is live, planned, experimental, or conceptual.

### Workflow Suitability Matters

Not every asset belongs in every workflow.

The product should preserve broad knowledge while refusing unsupported workflow use. Hiding ineligible assets from a workflow is not deletion; it is responsibility.

### Platforms Over Features

New product surfaces should strengthen the platform, not become isolated experiences.

Inventory, collection, portfolio, alerts, marketplace integrations, and AI assistant surfaces should reuse the same foundation of identity, evidence, intelligence, and decision support.

## Product Standard

Before product work begins, ask:

- Does this help users make better decisions?
- Does it expose enough evidence to support trust?
- Does it reduce friction without hiding uncertainty?
- Does it preserve human decision ownership?
- Does it belong to the platform, or is it an isolated feature?
