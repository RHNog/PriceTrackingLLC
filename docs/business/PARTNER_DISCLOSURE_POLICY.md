# Partner Disclosure Policy

This document defines what Project Phronesis may share externally during partnership conversations.

Project Phronesis is the internal engineering initiative responsible for building an evidence-driven decision intelligence platform for collectible markets. It is not the selected commercial brand.

## Core Policy

Implementation details remain confidential while business value remains transparent.

External conversations should communicate:

- What problem the platform solves.
- Why the platform benefits users and partners.
- How the platform behaves responsibly.
- What kind of partnership is being explored.

External conversations should not expose:

- Core algorithms.
- Calibration.
- Internal heuristics.
- Benchmark datasets.
- Proprietary repositories.
- Provider observation libraries.
- Negotiation or strategy logic.

## What May Be Shared Publicly

Publicly shareable:

- Vision and mission.
- High-level problem statement.
- Project Phronesis as engineering initiative.
- The fact that the commercial product name is undecided.
- Evidence-driven decision intelligence positioning.
- Responsible API usage philosophy.
- General platform categories: identity, provider observations, cached intelligence, assessment, business context, decision support.
- Customer value: better decision quality, explainability, transparency, and efficiency.
- Broad partnership goals.

Avoid presenting Project Phronesis as a commercial product brand.

## What May Be Shared During First Conversations

In initial partner conversations, share:

- Partnership purpose.
- High-level integration goals.
- High-level architecture categories.
- Responsible API practices.
- The existence of provider replay, repository caching, coverage-aware refresh, targeted provider requests, and diagnostics as API-conservation systems.
- Non-sensitive examples of user value.
- Mutual value model.
- Ecosystem benefits.

Keep these discussions conceptual. Do not reveal implementation specifics.

## What Requires An NDA

Consider an NDA before sharing:

- Detailed integration architecture.
- Partner-specific technical workflows.
- API usage estimates.
- Non-public roadmap details.
- Internal provider evaluation notes.
- Detailed caching behavior.
- Detailed repository lifecycle.
- Any partner-provided confidential information.

Even under NDA, disclose only the minimum necessary information.

## What Should Never Be Disclosed Externally Without Explicit Strategic Approval

Do not disclose:

- Market Intelligence algorithms.
- Asset Assessment methodology.
- Calibration weights.
- Negotiation logic.
- Strategy logic.
- Weighting models.
- Internal heuristics.
- Benchmark datasets.
- Historical repository contents.
- Provider observation library.
- Replay implementation details.
- Evidence scoring formulas.
- Outcome calibration methods.
- Any security-sensitive credentials, tokens, or operational secrets.

## TCGplayer-Specific Disclosure Guidance

Appropriate to share:

- Project Phronesis is an internal engineering initiative.
- The platform is an evidence-driven decision intelligence system for collectible markets.
- The platform seeks to complement TCGplayer by improving seller and buyer decision quality.
- The platform includes responsible API usage systems at a high level.
- Repeated user activity does not necessarily generate repeated provider requests.
- Public value includes explainable market intelligence, marketplace transparency, reduced provider load, and ecosystem health.

Do not share:

- Market Intelligence algorithms.
- Assessment Engine internals.
- Calibration and weighting models.
- Negotiation or strategy logic.
- Benchmark datasets.
- Replay implementation internals.

## Disclosure Review Checklist

Before sharing partner materials, confirm:

- Does this document identify Project Phronesis as the engineering initiative, not the product brand?
- Does this document avoid proprietary algorithms and internal heuristics?
- Does this document explain business value clearly?
- Does this document avoid confidential provider, repository, and benchmark details?
- Does this document fit the intended classification from `IP_STRATEGY.md`?
- Is an NDA needed before sending?
- Is this the minimum necessary disclosure for the conversation?

## Escalation Rule

If a partner requests details beyond public or partner-safe information, pause the conversation and classify the request before responding.

When unsure, treat the information as Internal or Proprietary.
